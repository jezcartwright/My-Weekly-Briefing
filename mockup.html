How the alignment works

JS measures the active tab's centre point, then translates the label so its centre matches. The label's natural starting position is the left edge of the band, so the shift amount is the distance from "left edge of band + label width/2" to "tab centre".

Clamped so the label never overflows the right edge of the band — if a tab is near the right of the strip, the label stops at the right edge rather than going off-screen.

Smooth 0.35s transition matches the ribbon's colour-change timing so they animate together.

On the live iPhone where six tabs scroll, the active tab's position is measured relative to the visible viewport when activated. The label aligns to where the user just tapped.
