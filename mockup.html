What I see comparing the three

A (current): The label floating mid-band looks orphaned at this width. The connection to anything specific isn't visible.

B (flush left, page margin): Clean editorial feel. Label aligns with the cards below — strong section-heading relationship. Doesn't track the tab cluster, but the cluster is centred and decorative anyway; the label tracks the CONTENT.

C (aligned to cluster): Visually links the label to the tabs but indents it deeply on wide screens, leaving a big empty band to its left.

My recommendation: B. It mirrors how editorial publications work — section headings always sit at the same left edge as the content of that section. The eye reads "LEADERSHIP" then drops down to "01 The 50-Year Career Problem" — perfectly aligned.

On phone (width below 720px), nothing changes — labels stay axis-aligned to the Leadership pill (the current behaviour). This is a wider-screen-only adjustment.
