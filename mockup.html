How to pick from this page

Section A — compression

The trade-off is real estate vs ceremony. C0 has full ceremony but takes ~200px before the reader sees a topic. C5 saves the most space but loses the editorial feel. C4 (sticky shrinking) is the best of both worlds technically but is the most engineering work.

My recommendation: C2 (inline logo) or C4 (sticky shrinking). C2 is dense and editorial-looking with no engineering complexity. C4 is best UX if you want the ceremonial banner on landing but compact reading.

Section B — tab centring

The After version is the fix. It centres when the row fits, scrolls when it doesn't, never clips the first or last pill.

Section C — menu font

Inter (M1) is fine but generic. IBM Plex Serif (M2) feels more like Weekly Briefing's voice. M3 is the most distinctive but might feel a bit overdesigned for a menu.

My recommendation: M2 IBM Plex Serif. Ties the menu to the rest of the publication, distinguishes the experience from generic SaaS dropdowns.
