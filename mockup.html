What I see in the comparison

Left (A — current): the label is anchored to the edge, which made sense when there was a subtitle on the right balancing it. Now that the subtitle's gone, the label sits alone on the left and the right half of the band is empty.

Centred (B — proposed): the label becomes a section heading. It announces the category like a chapter title in a book. Reads more deliberately. The full-width band frames the title rather than just hosting it at an edge.

This is a one-line CSS change — justify-content:center on .ci-text — so it's low-risk and easy to revert if you change your mind.

If you want, I can also increase the font size a bit (currently 12px, could go to 14-16px) since centring it gives the label more presence. Or keep the size and just centre.
