Where the two designs differ

Design A · single masthead

  • One unified orange strip with controls and identity sharing the space
  • Feels like an app — Stratechery, Substack, modern web products
  • Compact: fewer pixels vertically
  • Date sits in the utility row as a working element, not as part of the masthead identity

Design B · three strips (FT-style)

  • Utility strip (thin, muted), masthead strip (identity only), nav strip (categories)
  • Feels like a publication — FT, Economist, NYT
  • The edition date becomes part of the identity — like "No. 23 · Tuesday, 2 June 2026" sitting under the wordmark, in italic, smaller
  • More vertical space (~30-40px taller on mobile), but feels more considered
  • The "No. 23" edition number is a nice editorial flourish — gives each weekly issue a sense of permanence and collectability

What to weigh

Pick Design A if: you want the product to feel like a modern tool — quick, app-like, efficient. Users get to content faster. Best if reading time is the main metric.

Pick Design B if: you want the product to feel like a serious publication — considered, weighty, collectible. Users feel like subscribers to something prestigious. Best if perception of quality is the main lever (relevant before a LinkedIn launch — first impressions matter).
